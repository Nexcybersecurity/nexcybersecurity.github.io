Passwords are no longer enough to protect your business. Phishing, credential stuffing, and password reuse make SMBs especially vulnerable to account takeovers.

What is MFA?

Multi-Factor Authentication requires users to provide two or more pieces of evidence (factors) to verify their identity. The most common setup is a password (something you know) plus a code from your phone (something you have).

Why It Matters for SMBs

  • Stops 99% of account hacks: Even if passwords are stolen, attackers can‚Äôt log in without the second factor.
  • Easy to deploy: Tools like Duo, Okta, and Google Authenticator make setup quick.
  • Low cost, high impact: MFA is one of the cheapest but most effective security controls available.

Real-World Example

A small accounting firm adopted MFA across email and cloud storage. Within 3 months, they blocked multiple attempted logins from overseas IP addresses ‚Äî attempts that would have succeeded without MFA.
